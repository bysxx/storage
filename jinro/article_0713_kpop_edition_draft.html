JINRO's Toad Just Made Its K-Pop Debut

The new JINRO K-POP Edition puts idol-concept labels on Green Grape for one summer only — and its bottle-cap challenge is about to take over your feed.

JINRO's iconic toad is making its K-pop debut. The new JINRO K-POP Edition dresses JINRO Green Grape in idol-concept labels for one summer only, dropping across 21 countries — and from late July, creators across Asia and the US start posting its bottle-cap challenge. Here's the design story behind the bottles, and where to catch them.

A two-member debut

The concept reads like a debut stage. On the green bottle, the Toad fronts the stage in a black-and-white leather jacket with silver chains, lightstick raised to the crowd. The pink bottle answers with a second member — a pink toad in a jeweled black stage jacket and ribbon, striking her own pose. Both stand against an LED wall of concert lights, each holding a bunch of green grapes like a fan gift.

One thing worth being clear about: this is not a collaboration with any specific idol or group. It's JINRO's own mascot reimagined as one — a concept edition, which is part of the charm. And because the labels are designed as a pair — same stage, two members — the natural move is to keep one of each.

What's inside, and what comes with it

The soju is JINRO Green Grape, the best-selling flavor in JINRO's global fruit lineup of six — Grapefruit, Green Grape, Plum, Strawberry, Peach, and Lemon. The edition doesn't change the drink; it changes everything around it.

That includes actual fan merch. Alongside the bottles, JINRO is releasing two K-POP Edition Toad figurines — the Toad in full stage outfit, lightstick up, standing on a base stamped JINRO K-POP EDITION — plus bottle-shaped microphones, all in limited runs.

The bottle-cap challenge

Every cap in the edition hides a letter printed inside. The play is simple: collect caps, line the letters up into your own word or message, and post it. It turns the one part of the bottle everyone throws away into the part people photograph.

And it's about to be everywhere. From July 20 through the end of August, creators across China, Japan, Indonesia, Vietnam, Cambodia, and the United States are unboxing K-POP Edition kits — the two bottles, the figurine, the bottle mic — and filming their own takes on the challenge. If a toad in a leather jacket shows up on your feed this summer, now you know why.

Where to find it

The JINRO K-POP Edition began rolling out in late June across 21 countries, including the United States, Japan, and China, and runs through the peak summer season only, with quantities limited by market. If you spot the green or pink bottle at your local Korean grocery store or wherever JINRO fruit soju is sold, that's your window.
