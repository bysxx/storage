World Cup 2026 Starts June 11 - Here's the Match-Night JINRO Plan

For the next six weeks it is the trend everything else schedules around. The draw is done, so here is the quick schedule check, plus the easy JINRO pour for match night.

The match calendar is back in the group chat.

It is the biggest sporting event on the planet, and this summer it is being co-hosted across the US, Mexico, and Canada. The FIFA World Cup 2026 kicks off on Thursday, June 11 in Mexico City, with host Mexico facing South Africa at Estadio Azteca, the first stadium ever to host three World Cup openers (1970, 1986, and now 2026). From there it runs until the final on Sunday, July 19 at New York New Jersey Stadium, the MetLife Stadium just outside New York.

The quick schedule check

Here is the whole tournament in four lines.

  • Opening match: June 11 - Mexico vs South Africa, Estadio Azteca, Mexico City
  • Tournament window: June 11 to July 19, 2026 (39 days)
  • Format: 48 teams, 104 matches, 12 groups, 16 host cities, 3 countries - the first 48-team World Cup
  • Final: July 19 - New York New Jersey Stadium

Lock that in first. After that, the smart move is to follow the official FIFA schedule for kickoff times and knockout-round updates, especially if you are planning a watch party around a specific team.

June is the group-stage month

The whole group stage runs June 11 to 27, so June is where the watch-party calendar fills up and you get your first look at all 48 teams. The two openers most people on this side of the world will circle land on June 12: USA vs Paraguay at SoFi Stadium in Los Angeles, and Canada vs Bosnia and Herzegovina at BMO Field in Toronto.

The heavyweights are all in action through the month too. Brazil open against Morocco on June 13, England face Croatia on June 17, and Argentina, France, and Spain all play their group games before the knockouts begin. The new Round of 32 then kicks in at the end of the month, on June 28.

How the road to July 19 runs

Once the groups wrap, the bracket moves fast: Round of 32 from June 28 to July 3, Round of 16 from July 4 to 7, quarter-finals July 9 to 11, semi-finals July 14 and 15, the third-place match on July 18, and the final on July 19. One practical note: kickoffs span four US time zones plus Mexico and Canada, so a single match can be a lunchtime, afternoon, or late-night watch depending on the city. Check your local start time before you plan the table.
