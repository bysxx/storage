The Viral Wraps and Sandwiches of 2026 — and the JINRO Pour for Each

FoodTok is wrap-coded right now: crispy, hand-held, salty, and meant to be shared. Which means it's basically anju with a new name.

Scroll FoodTok for five minutes in 2026 and you'll get the same shape over and over: something crispy, hand-held, sliced in half to show the cross-section, with a sauce on the side. Wraps, grinders, crunchy flatbreads, and chopped sandwiches are the dominant format right now — and not by accident. They're built for short-form video, they're built for sharing, and they're built for snacking.

Which makes them, accidentally, perfect anju (안주) — the Korean word for the food you eat while drinking. Here are five viral wraps and sandwiches doing the rounds on FoodTok, and the JINRO pour that fits each one.

1. Chicken Caesar Wrap

The wrap that more or less restarted the trend. A grilled or fried chicken cutlet, romaine, Caesar dressing, parmesan, and sometimes a crispy element like crouton crumbs or brown rice crisps — all rolled tight into a tortilla and pressed until the outside crackles.

Why it's anju: Salty parmesan, garlicky dressing, fried chicken — a flavor stack that loves a clean dry sip between bites.

JINRO pour: JINRO Chamisul Fresh — clean dry finish cuts through the creamy Caesar.

2. Crispy Shawarma Wrap

The baton-shaped wrap that took over FoodTok this past year. Marinated chicken or beef, toum (garlic sauce), pickles, chili crema, all wrapped tight and grilled or pressed until the outside is fully crisped. The cross-section reveal is the whole shot.

Why it's anju: Garlic, spice, acid, crunch. It hits every drinking-food note in a single bite.

JINRO pour: JINRO Grapefruit — the citrus cuts the richness and matches the toum's brightness.

3. Grinder Sandwich

The chopped Italian sub that went viral and stayed viral. Deli meats, provolone, lettuce, banana peppers, and a chopped Italian dressing salad all tossed together so every bite has the same ratio — then tucked into a hoagie roll.

Why it's anju: Cured meats, pickled heat, sharp cheese. Classic salt-and-acid combo that drinkers have been pairing with alcohol for centuries.

JINRO pour: JINRO Is Back — bolder soju to match the deli-meat depth.

4. Cottage Cheese Flatbread

The high-protein flatbread that turned a single-ingredient hack into a year-long trend. Cottage cheese blended smooth, spread thin, baked, topped however you want — usually with a savory load (everything bagel seasoning, hot honey, salami, eggs).

Why it's anju: Salty toppings, crispy base, easy to cut into small shareable pieces. Reads like a Korean jeon (savory pancake) reimagined for the protein-trend era.

JINRO pour: JINRO Green Grape — gentle fruit sweetness sits well with savory cottage cheese.

5. Crunchwrap (Homemade)

The Taco Bell shape, but homemade and stuffed with anything — birria, smashed burger, Korean bulgogi, you name it. A tortilla folded into a hexagonal pocket with a crispy tostada layer inside, then seared on a flat-top until the outside is golden.

Why it's anju: Crunch outside, melt inside, eaten with hands, made for a group split. Exactly the rhythm soju asks for.

JINRO pour: JINRO Strawberry — the gentle fruit sweetness contrasts the savory crunch without competing.

The Throughline

None of these went viral because they're "soju food." They went viral because they're crispy, hand-held, easy to film, and easy to share — which is the same description Korea has used for anju for the better part of a century. The format is doing the work. The pairing is just the part TikTok hasn't named yet.
