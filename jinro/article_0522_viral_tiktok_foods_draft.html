5 Viral TikTok Foods of 2026 That Are Secretly Great Anju

FoodTok in 2026 is salty, crunchy, hand-held, and surprisingly drinking-friendly. Here are five viral foods doing the rounds — and the JINRO pour that fits each one.

Most "viral foods of the year" lists are basically dessert lists. This isn't that. The foods showing up most on FoodTok in 2026 are salty, savory, and built for hand-eating — which makes them, accidentally, perfect anju. Anju (안주) being the Korean word for the food you eat while drinking. Here are five worth knowing, and the JINRO pour for each.

Image: 2026 FoodTok in one frame — salty, savory, snackable, and somehow already pairing itself with soju.
1. Birria Tacos (Global)

They've been on FYP for two years and they're still going. Slow-braised beef in a deep, smoky chili broth, tucked into a crispy fried tortilla, with the broth on the side for dipping. The whole point is texture contrast — crunch outside, fall-apart meat inside.

Why it's anju: It's hand-held, slow to eat, and the dipping broth keeps the meal stretched out — exactly the rhythm soju asks for.

JINRO pour: JINRO Chamisul Fresh. The clean dry finish cuts through the rich beef fat without competing with the chili.

2. Gyoza Chips (Japan)

A 2026 Japan FoodTok favorite — wonton or gyoza wrappers fried into thin, crispy chips, dusted with shichimi or yuzu salt, and served with a tare dipping sauce. Snackable, photogenic, and absolutely meant for a group table.

Why it's anju: Salt, crunch, dipping sauce. The shichimi heat does the same job a kick of gochugaru does in Korean anju.

JINRO pour: JINRO Chamisul Fresh or JINRO Green Grape — both keep the palate light enough to keep eating.

3. Sisig Tacos & Sisig Rice Bowls (Philippines)

Sisig — the chopped, sizzling pork dish that's already a national bar food in the Philippines — is going viral again in 2026 through hybrid formats. Sisig tacos, sisig rice bowls, sisig sliders. Same chopped, citrus-spiked, savory base, new vessel.

Why it's anju: Sisig was literally invented as drinking food. The viral remixes just make it easier to film and share.

JINRO pour: JINRO Is Back. The slightly higher ABV and classic profile match sisig's punch better than the fruit lineup does.

4. Bánh Tráng Trộn (Vietnam)

A Vietnamese street food classic having a second viral moment in 2026 — rice paper strips tossed with dried shrimp, beef jerky, quail egg, mango, herbs, fried shallots, lime, and chili. It's chewy, sour, spicy, salty, and a little sweet. All at once.

Why it's anju: It hits every drinking-food note at once. Hand-eaten, slow to finish, built for a group of friends sharing one bag.

JINRO pour: JINRO Strawberry or JINRO Plum — the gentle fruit sweetness sits well with the lime-chili tang.

5. Tinned Fish Boards (Global)

The "girl dinner" cousin that refused to leave. Tinned sardines, anchovies, or mussels, plated with crackers, pickled vegetables, hot honey, and good olive oil. Aesthetic, fast to make, and on every FoodTok creator's account at least once this year.

Why it's anju: Briny, salty, slow-grazing food. This is the closest a Western trend has come to a classic Korean anju board in years.

JINRO pour: JINRO Chamisul Fresh — clean and dry, the same way you'd drink soju with grilled fish in Korea.

The Throughline

None of these went viral because they're "soju food." They went viral because they're shareable, salty, and slow to finish. Which is the same description Korea has been using for anju for the better part of a century. The trend is the food. The pairing is just the part TikTok hasn't figured out yet.
