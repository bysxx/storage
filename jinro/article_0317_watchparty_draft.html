Everything Worth Watching on Netflix This March—And What to Sip While You Stream

Netflix's March slate is busy, but these four releases stand out for very different reasons: romance, adventure, obsession, and dread. Here's what caught our attention—and what we'd pour alongside each one.

Boyfriend on Demand — Jisoo Leads Netflix's Newest Romance Series

Jisoo stars as an overworked webtoon producer who signs up for a virtual dating service—only to find the boundary between simulation and real life getting blurry fast. Seo In-guk plays the work rival who complicates things further. If your weeknight watchlist needs something breezy but still polished, this is probably the month's most accessible K-drama entry. All ten episodes began streaming March 6. Pair it with JINRO Strawberry Soju for something light and playful.

· · ·

One Piece: Into the Grand Line — Season 2 Arrives March 10

Season 2 sends the Straw Hats into the Grand Line, widening the world and raising the scale in a way fans have been waiting for. New characters, unfamiliar islands, and tougher enemies arrive as Luffy's crew pushes deeper into the legendary stretch of sea. Netflix is backing the launch with theatrical screenings for the first two episodes across the US, Canada, and Japan. This is the month's big communal watch—the kind of release built for fans who want scale, momentum, and a familiar crew to return to. All eight episodes drop at once on March 10.

Best saved for a weekend session. A cold Chamisul Fresh fits the marathon energy.

· · ·

Vladimir — Rachel Weisz in a Subversive Campus Comedy-Drama

Adapted from Julia May Jonas's bestselling novel, Vladimir centers on a literature professor (Rachel Weisz) whose obsession with a younger colleague (Leo Woodall) spirals into something she can't control. The series leans into dark comedy and twisty character work as much as tension—closer to a subversive campus satire than a straight thriller. All eight episodes arrived on March 5. If you're drawn to sharp, character-driven stories that sit somewhere between uncomfortable and funny, this one's worth the queue.

Chamisul Original, neat. Matches the mood.

· · ·

Something Very Bad Is Going to Happen — A New Wedding Horror Series, Executive Produced by the Duffer Brothers

Created and showrun by Haley Z. Boston, this eight-episode horror series follows a bride (Camila Morrone) with a growing sense that something terrible is going to happen at her wedding—and the closer the day gets, the worse it feels. Director Weronika Tofilska (Baby Reindeer) brings an atmospheric, slow-building dread that favors tension over jump scares. If you prefer horror that gets under your skin, this looks like the one to watch. It arrives on March 26.
