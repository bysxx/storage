Boyfriend on Demand — Jisoo's K-Drama Debut Everyone's Talking About

BLACKPINK's Jisoo stars as Seo Mi-Rae, a burnt-out webtoon producer who has zero time (or energy) to date. Her solution? A virtual reality dating app called Boyfriend on Demand. What starts as an easy escape quickly gets complicated when the line between her virtual romance and real-life sparks with a work rival (Seo In-guk) starts to blur.

It's lighthearted, a little chaotic, and very K-drama in the best way. Ten episodes, already streaming—so if you're looking for something fun and easy to binge on a weeknight, this is your pick.

One Piece Season 2 — The Grand Line Is Finally Here

After the massive success of Season 1, Luffy and the Straw Hat crew are back—and this time they're heading into the Grand Line. The legendary stretch of sea promises bigger threats, stranger islands, and a whole new lineup of characters. Netflix even put the first two episodes in theaters across the US, Canada, and Japan. That's how confident they are about this one.

If you watched Season 1, you already know the vibe: big action, genuine heart, and a surprisingly faithful adaptation that even the most skeptical anime fans came around to. Season 2 picks up right where it left off and goes harder.

Vladimir — Rachel Weisz at Her Most Unpredictable

Based on Julia May Jonas's bestselling novel, Vladimir follows a middle-aged literature professor (Rachel Weisz) whose life unravels when she develops an all-consuming obsession with her younger colleague (Leo Woodall). It's sharp, uncomfortable, and impossible to look away from.

This isn't feel-good TV. It's the kind of series you watch in a dim room with your phone face-down, fully locked in. Weisz's performance alone makes it one of the most talked-about drops of the month.

For a drama this intense, you want something that matches the mood. Chamisul Original, served neat, is the move. It's smooth, direct, and carries just enough weight to feel like the right companion for a slow-burn thriller. No mixers needed.

Something Very Bad Is Going to Happen — Wedding Horror from the Stranger Things Team

The Duffer Brothers (yes, the Stranger Things ones) are executive producing a new horror series, and the title pretty much tells you what you're getting into. A bride (Camila Morrone) has a growing feeling that something horrifying is going to happen at her wedding—and with each passing day, things get worse.

Director Weronika Tofilska (Baby Reindeer) sets the tone: slow, atmospheric dread that creeps up on you. If you're into horror that gets under your skin rather than jumping at you, mark March 26 on your calendar.
