A Sweet K-Drama for Easy Weeknight Bingeing

Boyfriend on Demand follows an overworked webtoon producer who signs up for a virtual dating service—only to find the boundary between simulation and real life getting blurry fast. If your weeknight watchlist needs something breezy but still polished, this is probably the month's most accessible K-drama entry. All ten episodes began streaming March 6. Adventure × Chamisul Fresh

The Big Weekend Marathon Watch

One Piece: Into the Grand Line sends the Straw Hats into uncharted waters, widening the world and raising the scale in a way fans have been waiting for. New characters, unfamiliar islands, and tougher enemies arrive as the crew pushes deeper into the legendary stretch of sea. Dark Comedy × Chamisul Original

A Sharp, Subversive Campus Story

Adapted from Julia May Jonas's bestselling novel, Vladimir centers on a literature professor whose obsession with a younger colleague spirals into something she can't control. The series leans into dark comedy and twisty character work as much as tension—closer to a subversive campus satire than a straight thriller. If you're drawn to sharp, character-driven stories that sit somewhere between uncomfortable and funny, this one's worth the queue. All eight episodes arrived on March 5. Chamisul Original, neat—it matches the mood.

Horror × Green Grape Soju

Slow-Burn Dread for Late-Night Viewing

Created and showrun by Haley Z. Boston, Something Very Bad Is Going to Happen follows a bride with a growing sense that something terrible will happen at her wedding—and the closer the day gets, the worse it feels. Executive produced by the Duffer Brothers and directed by Weronika Tofilska (Baby Reindeer), the series favors atmospheric tension over jump scares. If you prefer horror that gets under your skin, this looks like the one to watch. Eight episodes arrive on March 26.
