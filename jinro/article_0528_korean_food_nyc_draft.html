Best Korean Food in NYC: 6 Spots That Also Pour JINRO

Bapsang, BBQ, late-night anju, and a JINRO lineup that goes with all of it. Here's where to eat Korean in New York right now.

The best Korean food in NYC isn't just barbecue anymore. Manhattan's Koreatown still anchors the scene, but the action is also in Queens, in modern Seoul-coded dining rooms, and in late-night spots that pour JINRO Chamisul, JINRO Is Back, Ilpoom Jinro, and the full fruit lineup. Here are six restaurants worth knowing — each with confirmed JINRO or Chamisul on the menu.

Image: Korean dinner in NYC — bapsang, grilled meat, banchan, and JINRO on the table.
1. Gammeeok

Manhattan Koreatown · Korean classics

A K-Town anchor for the kind of Korean dinner you'd actually have in Seoul. Order the LA Galbi, the bulgogi, or the spicy pork BBQ, and ask for the soju menu — they pour Jinro Is Back, Ilpoom Jinro, and the fruit soju lineup.

2. Her Name Is Han

Manhattan Koreatown · Bapsang & home-style

Bapsang-style sets that lean into traditional home cooking rather than tabletop BBQ. The LA Galbi Bapsang, Spicy Pork BBQ Bapsang, and Mini Budae Hot Pot Bapsang are the headliners. Chamisul Soju and Ilpoom Jinro are both on the menu.

3. Hojokban New York

Manhattan · Modern Seoul-coded dining

A Seoul-pocha-meets-modern-dining feel. Hojok Galbi, Rosé Dak Galbi, and Tiger Budae Jjigae anchor the menu. Pairs with JINRO Is Back and Saero on the drink list.

4. Osamil

Manhattan · Late-night Korean dining

Open-late Korean dining for the kind of dinner that turns into drinks. The Korean Market Fried Chicken, Kimchi Fried Rice Steak, Uni Bibimbap, and Johnson Tang are the menu highlights. Drinks lean broad — Chamisul Fresh, Chamisul Classic, JINRO Is Back, Grapefruit, and Green Grape.

5. Oncheon NYC

Manhattan · Soup, jeon & suyuk

The Korean spot for nights where the food is broth-led. Korean Traditional Shabu-Shabu, Seafood Kimchi Jeon, Su-yuk, and the Chanhap Meal Box are all built for slow drinking. Soju options: Chamisul Fresh and Chamisul Original.

6. New York K-BBQ AYCE

Queens / Flushing · Group BBQ + fruit soju

The group-dinner option. LA Galbi, Beef Brisket, Spicy Pork Belly, Pork Jowl on the grill, and the widest fruit soju selection on this list — Chamisul Fresh, Jinro Is Back, Green Grape, Peach, and Apple. Easy to chain a karaoke night onto.

How to Plan the Night

A few pairing shortcuts if you don't want to think about it:

  • LA Galbi + Ilpoom Jinro — classic, premium, no surprises.
  • Korean fried chicken + Chamisul Fresh — clean dry soju cuts through the fry.
  • Budae jjigae + Jinro Is Back — bolder soju for a bolder stew.
  • Spicy pork belly + Green Grape — fruit soju cools the heat without flattening it.

If you want to eat Korean food in NYC the way New Yorkers actually do, order the soju too. The pairing is half the meal.
