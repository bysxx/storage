Best Korean Food in NYC: 6 Spots That Also Pour JINRO

Bapsang, BBQ, late-night anju, and a JINRO lineup that goes with all of it. Here's where to eat Korean in New York right now.

If you want the best Korean food in NYC, don't just look for barbecue. The real experience is bigger than that — soups, bapsang, late-night anju, premium soju, flavored bottles, and a night that can keep going right into karaoke. Manhattan's Koreatown still anchors the scene, but plenty of it now runs into Queens and into modern, Seoul-coded dining rooms. Here are six spots worth knowing — each one with JINRO or Chamisul confirmed on the menu.

1. Gammeeok

Manhattan Koreatown · Korean classics

Start here if you want the version of Korean dinner you'd actually have in Seoul. Gammeeok keeps the classics straight — LA galbi, bulgogi, spicy pork BBQ — and the soju menu runs deep. Ask for it and you'll find Jinro Is Back, Ilpoom Jinro, and the fruit soju lineup.

2. Her Name Is Han

Manhattan Koreatown · Bapsang & home-style

Han skips the tabletop grill and builds the meal around bapsang — full home-style sets with rice and banchan instead of a smoking grill in the middle of the table. The LA Galbi Bapsang, Spicy Pork BBQ Bapsang, and Mini Budae Hot Pot Bapsang are the ones to order. Chamisul and Ilpoom Jinro are both on the list.

3. Hojokban New York

Manhattan · Modern Seoul-coded dining

This is the one that feels like a Seoul pocha that grew up. Hojok Galbi, Rosé Dak Galbi, and Tiger Budae Jjigae anchor a menu that reads modern without losing the late-night energy. JINRO Is Back sits on the drink list to match it.

4. Osamil

Manhattan · Late-night Korean dining

Osamil is the late one — the dinner that quietly turns into drinks. Korean Market Fried Chicken, Kimchi Fried Rice Steak, Uni Bibimbap, and Johnson Tang carry the menu, and the soju selection is the broadest in Manhattan on this list: Chamisul Fresh, Chamisul Classic, JINRO Is Back, Grapefruit, and Green Grape.

5. Oncheon NYC

Manhattan · Soup, jeon & suyuk

For a night built on broth instead of smoke, Oncheon is the pick. Korean Traditional Shabu-Shabu, Seafood Kimchi Jeon, Su-yuk, and the Chanhap Meal Box are slow dishes made for slow drinking — and that's the kind of meal a clean soju belongs at. Chamisul Fresh and Chamisul Original are the options here.

6. New York K-BBQ AYCE

Queens / Flushing · Group BBQ + fruit soju

Take the train to Flushing for the group dinner. LA galbi, beef brisket, spicy pork belly, and pork jowl go on the grill, all-you-can-eat, and the fruit soju run is the longest on this list — Chamisul Fresh, Jinro Is Back, Green Grape, Peach, and Apple. A karaoke stop afterward is more or less assumed.

How to Pair It

A few shortcuts, if you'd rather not think about the drink order:

  • LA galbi → Ilpoom Jinro. A premium cut, a premium pour.
  • Korean fried chicken → Chamisul Fresh. Clean and dry, it cuts straight through the fry.
  • Budae jjigae → Jinro Is Back. A bolder stew can take a bolder soju.
  • Spicy pork belly → Green Grape. A fruit soju cools the heat without flattening it.

If you want to eat Korean food in NYC the way New Yorkers actually do, order the soju with the food — not after it. The pairing is half the meal.
