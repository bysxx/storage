A Chinese Singer-Songwriter Wrote JINRO Its Own Song

Shen Chongchong, a singer-songwriter with 6.18 million followers on Douyin, wrote and produced "Play the Colors, Pop JINRO!" — an original music video that turns JINRO flavors into colors you can wear.

JINRO has its own song now.

Shen Chongchong, a Chinese singer-songwriter and one of Douyin's most followed music creators, wrote, produced, and filmed an original music video for JINRO called "Play the Colors, Pop JINRO!" And no, this is not a jingle dropped into someone else's video. It is a full song, made by an artist, built around one idea: every JINRO flavor has a color.

Meet Shen Chongchong

If you are not on Douyin, here is the short version. Shen Chongchong is a singer and music creator who built her name through concerts and televised singing competitions, and now has 6.18 million followers on Douyin, where her music regularly travels far beyond her own page.

This project is a creator collaboration in the fullest sense — the song, the concept, and the video all come from her.

The Concept: Every Flavor Gets a Color

If dopamine dressing videos have passed through your feed, you already know the format: muted outfit, quick mirror transition, full color. Shen runs that idea through JINRO. She starts in grey and white, then swaps into color after color — and each one is a flavor. Peach turns everything pink. Green Grape turns the room green. The original JINRO turns the screen blue.

How the Video Moves

The story is simple summer material. It opens with Shen cooling off the heat with a chilled JINRO, moves through the rapid-fire look changes, slides into a table game with friends as an icebreaker, breaks into a challenge dance, and lands on a toast straight into the camera.

The challenge dance is the part made for you. It is short, repeatable, and built to be copied — exactly the kind of choreography that fills a feed one duet at a time. If you have ever learned a dance from your phone, this one will take you about a minute.

The Chorus That Sticks

Fair warning: the hook stays with you. It is spelled out loud — "J-I-N-R-O, JINRO!" — with lyrics that run in Chinese and weave in Korean, and the flavor lines repeat in threes: peach is sweet, sweet, sweet; green grape is fresh, fresh, fresh. Two listens is usually all it takes.
