JINRO Global Agent Goes Live This June

Brands turning their fans into creators is one of 2026's defining moves, and JINRO's version goes live this June. Here's what the program is, what to expect, and where to follow the launch.

Your next JINRO mission is about to go live.

One of the clearest brand trends of 2026 is companies handing the mic to their fans, turning the people who already love a product into the ones who make content about it. JINRO's take on that move, JINRO Global Agent, is set to go live this June: a global supporter program for people who already enjoy JINRO and like turning a good table, trip, or night out into something worth posting. If you missed the earlier wave, this is your cue to keep an eye out.

So, what is JINRO Global Agent?

It is a supporter program, not a one-off collaboration. Selected agents take on monthly missions from JINRO and create content around them over roughly three months, mostly on Instagram and TikTok. The idea JINRO keeps coming back to is two-way: less a brand talking at people, more fans turning their own JINRO moments into stories other people actually want to watch.

So the bar is simple. You like JINRO, you like making things, and you know how to make a normal night look good on a feed.

The missions come in two layers

Some missions are global, built to carry one JINRO idea across markets at the same time. Others are local, shaped around the season, the food, and whatever is trending where you are, so the program looks a little different in Manila than it does in Tokyo, Ho Chi Minh City, or a backyard in the US. That is the part that keeps it current: the local missions move with each market's trends instead of running one fixed script.

In practice that means local tables, summer trips, get-togethers, and home gatherings, captured the way you would share them anyway, then pulled together into official channel recaps.

What agents actually get

Beyond the missions, agents get early access to new JINRO products, which is well-timed given how much JINRO is rolling out globally this year, from the matte-black JINRO Melon limited edition to the wider fruit lineup. There are rewards for completing missions, and for the standout creators, extra incentives and support to grow their own channels.

It leans less on follower count and more on people who are genuinely into JINRO and into making content. That is the whole point of a supporter program over a quick paid post.

Where to follow the launch

Full details, eligibility, and the application live on JINRO's official Agent page, and the program runs across a handful of global markets. When the June launch goes live, the official call and any updates will roll out through JINRO's global channels, so the easiest move is to follow along and check the Agent page when it opens.
