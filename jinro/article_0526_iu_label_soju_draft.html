IU Is on the Bottle Now: JINRO's IU Label Soju Lands in Vietnam and Taiwan

Soft K-pop visuals on the front, the same Chamisul inside. Here's the product, when it drops, and where to find it.

JINRO's long-running brand ambassador IU is finally getting her own bottle. The IU label edition of Chamisul rolls out in Vietnam and Taiwan in the fourth week of May 2026 — and yes, it's the same soju you already know, just wearing a softer, fan-friendly outfit.

Image: The IU label Chamisul edition launching in Vietnam and Taiwan, May 2026.
The Product

The IU label edition is a limited-run packaging refresh of JINRO Chamisul, the original clean and crisp Korean soju that's been the brand's anchor product since the beginning. The liquid inside the bottle is the same Chamisul you'd find at any K-BBQ table back in Seoul. What changes is the front of the bottle.

IU appears directly on the label — a clean, K-pop-meets-classic-soju look that swaps the usual green-and-white minimalism for something more collectible. For fans, it's the kind of bottle you keep after the soju is gone.

Quick product summary:

  • Product: JINRO Chamisul — IU Label Limited Edition
  • Inside: The classic Chamisul profile — clean, crisp, slightly sweet finish
  • Label: Special IU edition artwork (limited run)
The Launch Window

The IU label edition rolls out in the fourth week of May 2026. As with most JINRO limited editions, this is a fan-friendly volume — meaning once it sells through in each market, that's it for this run.

Where to Find It

The launch markets are Vietnam and Taiwan. You can expect the bottles to show up through the same channels Chamisul already lives in across those two countries — major supermarkets, Korean grocery stores, K-mart-style retailers, Korean restaurants and K-BBQ venues, and select online retail.

Availability differs by city and channel, and limited editions tend to move quickly, so the most accurate way to track it is straight from JINRO:

  • Instagram @jinro_global
  • YouTube @jinroglobal
  • Facebook @jinro.global
Why It Matters for Fans

IU has been Chamisul's face for years, so the IU label isn't a one-off stunt — it's the most direct expression yet of that long collaboration. For fans in Vietnam and Taiwan, it's also a low-effort way to pick up a piece of K-pop and K-drinking culture in one go: a bottle that doubles as merch, and merch that happens to be the most-poured soju in Korea.
