The Best Brand April Fools' Pranks of 2026 — And Yes, JINRO Played Too

Brands went absolutely unhinged this April 1st — and the line between prank and real product has never been blurrier. Here are the ones that broke everyone's feeds, and what JINRO dropped this year.

OK so every year we tell ourselves "I won't fall for any brand pranks this time." And every year, without fail, we end up doom-scrolling at 2 AM genuinely unable to tell what's real and what's not. This year was no different.

April Fools' 2026 was stacked. The production quality was genuinely shocking, the ideas were brilliant, and — here's the thing — people couldn't tell what was real anymore. Some "pranks" had fans desperately begging to buy them. Others turned out to be actual products all along. When a brand April Fools' campaign taps into something people genuinely want, it stops being a joke and starts being a product launch in disguise.

We picked the four that absolutely dominated everyone's feeds this year. Let's get into it.

1. Nissin Cup Noodles — The Heatless Noodle Hair Curler

Nissin announced a Cup Noodles Heatless Curls Kit — flexible foam rods colored to look like ramen noodles, paired with a setting spray called "Broth Boost Finishing Mist." The concept? Wrap them in your hair overnight and wake up with perfect ramen-wave curls. No heat, no hot water — just noodle energy. The packaging was so polished it looked like a legit beauty product drop.

Here's what made this land so hard: heatless curlers are genuinely one of the biggest ongoing beauty trends right now — the global market is projected to hit $248 million by 2032, and TikTok is still flooded with tutorials. So when Nissin dropped a noodle version, people genuinely could not tell. Instagram comments were flooded with "I would actually buy this" and "please make this real." It was a joke — but it tapped into something people already wanted, and that's exactly why it worked.

2. Dyson — The Pet Styling Range

Dyson announced a full Dyson Beauty Pet Range — and the video was chef's kiss. It opened with gorgeous, salon-quality styled hair that looked like it belonged in a luxury shampoo commercial. Then the camera pulled back to reveal… it was a poodle. And then a horse with a perfectly straightened mane. And then a cat with immaculately curled chest fluff. Each shot used Dyson's signature sleek, premium aesthetic — soft lighting, sophisticated copy, dead-serious tone — making it indistinguishable from an actual Dyson product launch.

That's what made this so effective. Dyson's real products are already so hyper-specific and premium-priced that a pet version felt genuinely plausible. A $500 hair dryer company making an Airwrap for cats? Honestly, it didn't even sound that far off. Pet influencers were reposting it unironically within hours, and the comments were split between "this can't be real" and "why isn't this real."

But here's where it gets interesting. Some brands took the opposite approach — using April Fools' Day to launch something completely real, knowing nobody would believe them. When the whole internet is primed to assume everything is fake, dropping a genuine product becomes the ultimate power move. Let's look at two brands that played this reverse card brilliantly.

3. Yahoo — The Scrōll Stoppr (This One Was Actually Real)

Yahoo dropped the Scrōll Stoppr — a tiny weighted ring you put on your thumb that makes it physically impossible to scroll. Sounds like a joke, right?

It was real. You could actually buy it for $4.99 on Yahoo's TikTok Shop, shipped in a box that plays the classic Yahoo yodel when you open it. A real product, sold on a real platform, for a real price — and it sold out fast. This is actually Yahoo's second year of this strategy; in 2025 they pulled the same move with the "Touch Grass" Keyboard.

Think about it: back in 2004, Google launched Gmail on April 1st offering 1GB of free storage — and everyone thought it was a prank. It wasn't. That moment proved that April Fools' Day is actually the perfect time to launch something real, because the disbelief itself becomes the marketing. Yahoo is running that exact playbook, 22 years later. Genius.

→ Read the full story on Muse by Clios or Creative Bloq.

4. Dunkin' — 1,000,001 Free Coffees (Again)

And speaking of brands that chose honesty over humor — Dunkin' did it again. Last year, they gave away 1,000,000 free coffees on April Fools' Day with the code "ThisIsNotAJoke." Half the internet didn't believe it. This year? They came back with 1,000,001 coffees — yes, exactly one more — just to prove they meant it.

Here's how it worked: on April 1st, Dunkin' Rewards members could enter the code "StillNotAJoke" in the app to claim a free hot or iced coffee, any size. One per person, valid until all 1,000,001 codes were gone. No fake products. No elaborate prank video. Just a massive, real giveaway on the one day when nobody believes anything.

That's what makes this so smart. The second year hits even harder because people know it's coming and still can't fully trust it. The skepticism IS the marketing.
