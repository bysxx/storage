The Best Brand April Fools' Pranks of 2026 — And Yes, JINRO Played Too

Noodle hair curlers. A pet Airwrap for poodles. A thumb ring that physically stops you from scrolling. Brands went absolutely unhinged this April 1st. Here are the best ones — and what JINRO dropped this year.

OK so every year we tell ourselves "I won't fall for any brand pranks this time." And every year we end up googling "is the Nissin hair curler real" at 2 AM. This year was no different.

April Fools' 2026 was stacked. Brands didn't just post a funny graphic and call it a day — they went full production, full commitment, and in some cases, full product launch. The line between "prank" and "please take my money" has never been thinner.

We picked the four that absolutely dominated everyone's feeds this year. Let's get into it.

1. Nissin Cup Noodles — The Heatless Noodle Hair Curler

Nissin announced a heatless hair curler kit shaped like, yes, actual cup noodles. The rods were noodle-colored, the setting spray was called "Broth Boost," and the packaging looked like it belonged in the beauty aisle at Target.

Here's the thing: heatless curlers are genuinely trending right now. So when Nissin dropped this, people genuinely did not know if it was real. The comments were a beautiful mess of "WHERE DO I BUY THIS" and "...this is a joke right?" It was a joke. But honestly? Someone's going to make this for real within six months. Calling it now.

2. Dyson — The Pet Styling Range

Dyson announced a full pet grooming line built on their existing beauty tech. An Airwrap for cats. An Airstrait for horse manes. A Supersonic dryer engineered specifically for "maximum poodle volume."

The reason this worked so well? Dyson's real products are already so hyper-specific that a pet version felt genuinely plausible. Like, this is a company that made a $500 hair dryer. A cat Airwrap doesn't even sound that far off. The internet absolutely ate it up, and pet influencers were reposting it unironically within hours.

3. Yahoo — The Scrōll Stoppr (This One Was Actually Real)

Yahoo dropped the Scrōll Stoppr — a tiny weighted ring you put on your thumb that makes it physically impossible to scroll. Sounds like a joke, right?

It was real. You could actually buy it for $4.99 on the Yahoo TikTok Shop. A real product, sold on a real platform, for a real price. On the one day nobody believes anything. The Gmail playbook, but make it 2026. Genius move.

4. Dunkin' — One Million Free Coffees (Again)

While every other brand was lying, Dunkin' once again chose violence by being completely honest. Over one million free coffees for Rewards members with the code "STILLNOTAJOKE."

No fake product. No elaborate prank video. Just a massive freebie on the one day people assume everything is a scam. This is the second year Dunkin' has run this play, and honestly? It works even better the second time because now people know it's coming and still can't tell if it's real. Anti-prank energy. We respect it deeply.
