JINRO Turned a Tokyo Club Green for One Night

Inside "Back to 20XX," the one-night party JINRO Chamisul threw with Tokyo street-culture media DROPTOKYO in Nakameguro — the room, the looks, and the green that took over both.

On the last Saturday of June, a club under the train tracks in Nakameguro went completely green.

The occasion was Back to 20XX, a one-night party by JINRO Chamisul and DROPTOKYO, held at HVEN in Tokyo. From ten at night until four in the morning, the venue filled with DJ sets, live acts, Y2K looks, and green bottles moving from hand to hand.

A One-Night Collaboration With DROPTOKYO

DROPTOKYO is the media channel that has spent years documenting Tokyo street fashion and the people who wear it, snap by snap. You can find their archive on Instagram at @drop_tokyo.

That is what made this party different from a standard brand event. Back to 20XX was built like a Tokyo night first — the lineup, the crowd, the dress code — with Chamisul running through all of it.

Inside the Room

HVEN sits under the elevated rail line in Nakameguro, and for this night it was dressed in green from the lights to the bar. Two rooms ran in parallel, with twelve DJ and live acts rotating across them until morning. Entry was open to guests twenty and over.

The bar leaned into the theme, too. Chamisul bottles covered in rhinestones sat on display next to a crystal-studded toad, and the shelves behind were stacked with green bottles end to end.

The Dress Code: Green and Y2K

The party's name points at the mood: somewhere between 2000 and a future Tokyo. Guests answered with baby tees, low-rise denim, chokers, bandanas, and green worked into almost every look — and DROPTOKYO's photographers walked the floor shooting street snaps all night, many of them with a Chamisul bottle in frame.

There was one more detail for the early crowd: the first hundred guests received an original Chamisul × DROPTOKYO bandana, made only for this night.

Soju in the Tokyo Night

Soju has been settling into Tokyo nightlife for a while now, and nights like this show what that actually looks like: not an import on a shelf, but a bottle in hand on a dance floor in Nakameguro.
