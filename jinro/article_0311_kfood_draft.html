Korea's Food Scene Has Moved On—Here's What Everyone's Eating Now

The bakery queues for Dubai Chewy Cookies might be getting shorter—because the obsession has moved straight to the bar. Seoul's food scene has already evolved, and the new wave looks nothing like what came before.

While the rest of the world was still discovering Dubai Chewy Cookies, Korea's food scene quietly evolved into something bigger. From a resurfaced 2008 mukbang clip that went massively viral to TikTok's latest frozen gummy ASMR trend—here's what's on everyone's plate in Seoul right now, and what to pour alongside it.

· · ·

No. 1 Trending

Bomdong Bibimbap — The 18-Year-Old Clip That Went Viral

Korean comedian Kang Ho-dong ate a bowl of bomdong bibimbap—a dish made with Korean spring cabbage, a tender, sweet variety of napa cabbage harvested in late winter—on KBS back in 2008. Fast forward to February 2026: that clip resurfaced and hit 6.3 million views. Overnight, bomdong went from a niche seasonal vegetable to Korea's most-searched ingredient. Google Trends jumped from 15 to 100. Online mentions grew 540%. Wholesale prices surged 78% as demand outpaced supply.

It's the face of Gen Z's "Seasonal Core" movement—a growing lifestyle trend around eating what's in peak season, locally sourced, and affordable. Fresh, healthy, photogenic, and gone by April.

Rising Fast

Frozen Gummies — The ASMR Snack Taking Over Feeds

Freeze a bag of gummy candy for four hours. The result crunches like ice but melts like candy—and the sound is deeply satisfying. Frozen gummy clips have racked up over 2 million views on Instagram. GS25 jelly sales jumped 65%. It's Korea's "modisumer" (modify + consumer) culture at work: take an existing product, reinvent it into something new, film it, share it.

Modisumer Hit

Buldak Seaweed Soup Ramen — The Remix That Set Sales Records

Samyang's Buldak fire noodles cooked in beef seaweed soup broth. One YouTube mukbang hit 1.6M views, and convenience store chain CU reported Buldak sales up 116%, with seaweed soup up 47.5%. The mild, savory broth softens the intense heat just enough, while the spicy noodles elevate the soup. Two things that seem like an unlikely pair—yet the combination works beautifully.

· · ·

Still Going Strong

Halmennial Desserts — Grandma's Recipes, Gen Z Approved

"Halmennial"—a blend of halmae (Korean for grandma) and millennial—has turned traditional Korean sweets into Seoul's most beloved dessert wave. Yakgwa, deep-fried honey cookies, are being tucked into croissants and layered over ice cream. Gaeseong juak, traditional rice flour donuts glazed in cinnamon syrup, are selling out at trendy cafés. The appeal? Simple ingredients, nothing artificial, and the signature kkudeok (soft, chewy) texture that Gen Z loves.

Honorable Mentions

Gil-gamja — The Street Classic That Never Fades

Some things don't need a viral moment to stay relevant. Gil-gamja (literally "street potato")—a gluten-free potato-and-tapioca fritter born in Gangneung's Jungang Market—still draws 2-hour lines in 2026. Crispy on the outside, impossibly chewy on the inside, and always served piping hot. While other street food trends come and go, this one endures.
