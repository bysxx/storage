Seoul's Already Onto the Next Thing—Here's What's on the Menu

While the rest of the world was still catching onto Dubai Chewy Cookies, Seoul quietly moved on. An 18-year-old mukbang clip just broke 6 million views, frozen gummies became the new ASMR aesthetic, and grandma's recipes are suddenly the coolest thing on the dessert menu. Here's what's actually on everyone's plate right now—and what to pour alongside it.

Bomdong Bibimbap — An 18-Year-Old Clip Just Changed Everything

Quick context: bomdong is Korean spring cabbage—sweeter and more tender than regular napa, only available in late winter. Back in 2008, comedian Kang Ho-dong ate a bowl of bomdong bibimbap on KBS. No one thought much of it. Then in February 2026, the clip resurfaced—and hit 6.3 million views. Google Trends for bomdong: 15 → 100. Online mentions: up 540%. Wholesale prices: up 78%.

Why now? It fits perfectly into what we're calling "Seasonal Core"—Gen Z's growing obsession with eating what's in peak season, locally sourced, and budget-friendly. It's fresh, it's photogenic, and it's gone by April. If you haven't tried it yet, the clock is ticking.

What to pour: JINRO is Back. Bomdong's delicate sweetness calls for something equally clean and understated—JINRO is Back's smooth, no-frills character lets the seasonal flavors do the talking. Pour it cold, keep it simple.

Frozen Gummies — Your New Favorite ASMR Snack

The recipe is almost too simple: freeze a bag of gummy candy for four hours. That's it. But the result—crunches like ice, melts like candy—sounds so satisfying that frozen gummy clips have pulled in over 2 million views on Instagram alone. GS25 jelly sales? Up 65%. Welcome to Korea's "modisumer" (modify + consumer) era: take a product off the shelf, remix it into something entirely new, film it, share it, repeat.

What to pour: JINRO Lemon Soju. Frozen gummies are usually chased with cider for that fizzy-sweet contrast. JINRO Lemon Soju hits the same refreshing note—citrus brightness that plays off the chewy, fruity texture perfectly.

Buldak × Seaweed Soup — The Mashup No One Expected

Samyang's Buldak fire noodles, cooked in beef seaweed soup broth. Sounds like it shouldn't work, right? One YouTube mukbang hit 1.6M views, and CU convenience stores reported Buldak sales up 116%, with seaweed soup up 47.5%. The savory broth dials down the heat just enough; the spicy noodles give the mild soup a serious upgrade. This is modisumer culture at its best—and honestly, once you try it, there's no going back.

What to pour: Chamisul Fresh. Spicy noodles need a palate reset, not more flavor. Chamisul Fresh at 16% is clean enough to cut through the heat, smooth enough to keep the meal going. The classic ramen-and-soju combo exists for a reason.

Halmennial Desserts — Grandma's Recipes Are the Main Character Now

Meet the "Halmennial" wave—halmae (Korean for grandma) meets millennial. Traditional Korean sweets are having a full-blown moment. Yakgwa (deep-fried honey cookies) are showing up inside croissants and on top of ice cream. Gaeseong juak (rice flour donuts glazed in cinnamon syrup) are selling out at Seoul's trendiest cafés. The common thread? Simple ingredients, nothing artificial, and that addictive kkudeok (soft, chewy) texture everyone keeps coming back for.

What to pour: JINRO Plum Soju. Traditional Korean sweets already lean into honey and sesame—JINRO Plum Soju's gentle, mellow sweetness fits right into that flavor family without competing. Think of it as the dessert soju that was made for this moment.

Gil-gamja — The One That Never Needs a Comeback

Not everything needs a viral moment. Gil-gamja (literally "street potato")—a gluten-free potato-and-tapioca fritter from Gangneung's Jungang Market—has been pulling 2-hour lines since before any of these trends existed. Crispy outside, impossibly chewy inside, always served piping hot. Trends come and go. Gil-gamja just stays.

What to pour: Chamisul Original. Hot, salty, crispy street food deserves the original. Chamisul Original at 20.1% has the backbone to match gil-gamja's bold, savory crunch—no frills, just a proper shot between bites.
