Who is Noa Bar?

Noa Bar is a Los Angeles-based music creator with a feed built around songs, videos, and performance clips. On Instagram, @heyimnoabar has grown past 80K followers, and one recent Reel has crossed the 1M-view mark. That matters here: this is not a random soundtrack placement, but a collaboration with an artist who already knows how to make music travel through a feed.

On Apple Music, her catalog includes the album DANCEABLE PROBLEMS, tracks like "DISCO(NNECT)," "Diagnosis," and "Broccoli," plus the YELLOW HOUSE SESSIONS release. The through-line is direct: danceable pop energy, clean hooks, and enough personality that the clips do not feel interchangeable.

The Tiny Desk signal

The other reason Noa Bar is worth knowing: out of more than 6,000 entries, NPR Music narrowed its 2026 Tiny Desk Contest to just five finalists — and Noa Bar is one of them.

Tiny Desk is NPR's live-performance series, filmed behind host Bob Boilen's actual desk at NPR's Washington, D.C. headquarters. The sets are deliberately stripped back — no studio polish, no backing tracks — so an artist's voice, musicianship, and stage presence carry the whole performance. Since 2008 it has featured everyone from global headliners to breakout independent acts, which is part of why a spot there carries real weight.

The Contest is the series' open call for independent, unsigned musicians, who submit a single live video of an original song. Noa Bar earned her spot with "Diagnosis," a live-looping piece that layers her vocals over her own percussion in real time. For a JINRO collaboration, that is a useful signal — she brings more than a good visual. She brings live-music credibility.
